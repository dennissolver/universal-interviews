## Required Parameters

### 1. Research Topic

The central subject or theme of your interview study.

**Examples:**
- "The First 5 Minutes of a VC Pitch"
- "Pain Points in Traditional Market Research"
- "Enterprise Software Buying Decisions"

**Best Practices:**
- Keep it focused and specific
- Frame it as a question or area of exploration
- Ensure it aligns with your research objectives

---

### 2. Target Interviewee Market

Define who you want to interview. Be specific about demographics, roles, and qualifying criteria.

**Parameters to specify:**
- **Industry/Sector:** Tech, Healthcare, Finance, etc.
- **Role/Title:** VCs, Product Managers, Marketing Directors, etc.
- **Company Size:** Startup, SMB, Enterprise
- **Experience Level:** Years in role, seniority
- **Geographic Focus:** Region, country, or global

**Example:**
> "Venture Capital partners and associates at early-stage funds (Seed to Series A), with 3+ years of experience evaluating pitches, based in North America."

---

### 3. AI Interviewer Persona

Customize your AI interviewer to match your research context.

#### Voice Selection

| Option | Voice | Characteristics |
|--------|-------|-----------------|
| **Female** | Sarah | Warm, professional, approachable |
| **Male** | Adam | Confident, deep, authoritative |

You can select this during your conversation with Sandra, or change it on the review screen.

#### Interviewer Name

Choose a name that fits your research context:
- **Casual/Friendly:** Alex, Jordan, Sam
- **Professional:** Dr. Taylor, Professor Chen
- **Industry-specific:** Whatever resonates with your audience

Sandra will ask: *"Would you prefer a male or female voice?"* and *"What would you like to name your interviewer?"*

---

### 4. AI Interviewer Tone

Set the conversational style for your AI interviewer. This affects how questions are asked and how the AI responds to participant answers.

**Tone Options:**

| Tone | Best For | Characteristics |
|------|----------|-----------------|
| **Friendly & Professional** | Most B2B research | Warm yet polished |
| **Professional** | Executive interviews | Formal, business-appropriate |
| **Casual & Friendly** | Consumer research | Relaxed, informal |
| **Academic** | Expert interviews | Precise, neutral, scholarly |
| **Empathetic** | Sensitive topics | Understanding, supportive, patient |

---

### 5. Interview Questions

The core questions that guide the interview conversation.

#### How It Works Now

1. **Tell Sandra your research goal** â€” What are you trying to learn?
2. **Sandra suggests questions** â€” Based on your objectives, she'll propose 5-10 questions
3. **Collaborate and refine** â€” Add, remove, modify, or request alternatives
4. **Review on screen** â€” See all questions laid out, reorder with drag handles
5. **Final edits** â€” Add more, remove any, tweak wording before creating

#### No Limits
Include as many questions as your research needs â€” there's no cap.

#### Question Structure (Recommended)

1. **Opening Question (1):** Easy, rapport-building question
2. **Core Questions (varies):** Main research questions
3. **Closing Question (1-2):** Wrap-up, additional thoughts

---

### 6. Research Objectives

Define what you want to learn and how the insights will be used. Sandra will use this to suggest relevant questions.

**Framework:**

```
Primary Objective:
[The main insight or answer you're seeking]

Secondary Objectives:
- [Supporting insight #1]
- [Supporting insight #2]

Intended Use:
[How will this research inform decisions?]
```

---

### 7. Participant Benefits & Incentives

Why should someone participate? Include in invitations and interview intro.

| Element | Description | Example |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| **Time Commitment** | How long it takes | "10-15 minutes" |
| **Value Exchange** | What they receive | "Early access to research findings" |
| **Community Impact** | How it helps others | "Shape resources for the founder community" |

---

### 8. Interview Duration

Set expectations for interview length.

| Questions | Estimated Duration | Best For |
|-----------|-------------------|----------|
| 5-7 | 8-12 minutes | Quick pulse checks |
| 8-12 | 12-18 minutes | Standard depth |
| 13-20 | 18-30 minutes | Deep dives |
| 20+ | 30+ minutes | Comprehensive studies |

## Example Panel Configuration

```yaml
Panel Name: "The First 5 Minutes - VC Pitch Research"

Research Topic: 
  What happens in the first 5 minutes of a startup pitch that 
  determines whether investors want a second meeting?

Target Market:
  - Role: VC Partners and Associates
  - Focus: Early-stage (Pre-seed to Series A)
  - Experience: 3+ years in VC
  - Geography: North America

AI Interviewer:
  Name: Rachel
  Voice: Female (Sarah)
  Tone: Friendly & Professional

Questions: 7 (collaboratively designed with Sandra)
  1. How many founder pitches would you estimate you've seen?
  2. Walk me through what's going through your mind in the first five minutes.
  3. What signals make you lean in versus check out?
  4. Is there something founders do that they think helps but actually hurts?
  5. How much does the deck matter versus how the founder presents?
  6. Can you recall a pitch where the first five minutes completely changed your expectations?
  7. If you could give founders one piece of advice about those opening minutes?

Duration: 12-15 minutes

Closing: "Thank you so much for sharing your perspective today."
```
